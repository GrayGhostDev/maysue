Occupational Therapy Services

Our occupational therapy services focus on helping individuals develop, recover, and improve the skills needed for daily living and working. Our skilled occupational therapists work with patients of all ages who have physical, sensory, or cognitive challenges that affect their ability to perform everyday activities.

Our Occupational Therapy Services Include:

  • Activities of Daily Living (ADL) Training: Assistance with essential self-care tasks such as bathing, dressing, grooming, and eating.
  • Instrumental Activities of Daily Living (IADL) Training: Support with complex daily tasks such as meal preparation, medication management, shopping, and home maintenance.
  • Fine Motor Skill Development: Exercises and activities to improve hand dexterity, coordination, and manipulation skills.
  • Cognitive Rehabilitation: Strategies to address challenges with memory, attention, problem-solving, and executive functioning.
  • Sensory Processing Intervention: Techniques to help individuals who have difficulty processing and responding to sensory information.
  • Adaptive Equipment Recommendations: Guidance on selecting and using tools and devices that can enhance independence and safety.
  • Home Modification Assessments: Evaluation of the home environment to identify and address potential barriers to independence and safety.

Our Approach

At May Sue Case Management, our occupational therapy approach is client-centered and holistic. We believe in addressing not only the physical aspects of function but also the emotional, social, and environmental factors that impact daily life. Our process includes:

  • Comprehensive Assessment: Thorough evaluation of your abilities, challenges, goals, and the environments in which you live, work, and socialize.
  • Collaborative Goal Setting: Working together to establish meaningful, achievable goals that reflect your priorities and needs.
  • Individualized Intervention: Development of a personalized treatment plan that may include therapeutic activities, adaptive strategies, environmental modifications, and education.
  • Family/Caregiver Training: Education and guidance for family members and caregivers to support your progress and independence.
  • Regular Progress Monitoring: Ongoing assessment of your advancement toward goals with adjustments to the treatment plan as needed.

Who Can Benefit from Occupational Therapy?

  • Individuals recovering from stroke or traumatic brain injury
  • People with developmental disabilities
  • Those with arthritis, multiple sclerosis, or other chronic conditions
  • Individuals with mental health conditions
  • Children with developmental delays or sensory processing issues
  • Older adults experiencing cognitive or physical changes
