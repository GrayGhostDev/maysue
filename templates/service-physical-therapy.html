Physical Therapy Services

Our physical therapy services are designed to help patients recover from injuries, manage chronic conditions, and improve overall physical function. Our experienced physical therapists work closely with each patient to develop personalized treatment plans that address their specific needs and goals.

Our Physical Therapy Services Include:

  • Orthopedic Rehabilitation: Treatment for musculoskeletal injuries, post-surgical recovery, and chronic conditions affecting bones, joints, muscles, and connective tissues.
  • Neurological Rehabilitation: Specialized therapy for patients with neurological conditions such as stroke, traumatic brain injury, Parkinson's disease, and multiple sclerosis.
  • Sports Injury Rehabilitation: Targeted treatment for athletes recovering from sports-related injuries, focusing on safe return to activity and injury prevention.
  • Pain Management: Evidence-based approaches to reduce pain and improve function, including manual therapy, therapeutic exercises, and modalities.
  • Balance and Fall Prevention: Assessment and treatment to improve balance, reduce fall risk, and enhance mobility in older adults and individuals with balance disorders.
  • Post-Surgical Rehabilitation: Specialized care following orthopedic, cardiac, or other surgeries to optimize recovery and restore function.

Our Approach

At May Sue Case Management, our physical therapy approach is patient-centered and evidence-based. We begin with a comprehensive evaluation to assess your condition, functional limitations, and goals. Based on this assessment, we develop a personalized treatment plan that may include:

  • Therapeutic Exercises: Customized exercise programs to improve strength, flexibility, endurance, and function.
  • Manual Therapy: Hands-on techniques to mobilize joints, reduce pain, and improve tissue mobility.
  • Modalities: Use of physical agents such as heat, cold, electrical stimulation, and ultrasound to manage pain and promote healing.
  • Functional Training: Activities that simulate daily tasks to improve your ability to perform routine activities.
  • Patient Education: Guidance on self-management strategies, home exercise programs, and injury prevention.
